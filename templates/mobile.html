Side Loading Apps

Smartphones allow you to install applications from third party sources (somewhere other than the app store) through a process called “side loading”. Third-party apps are attractive because they offer more variety than what the app store can provide. Since these applications are not screened by the app store, it is possible for app developers to embed malware into their application.

For more information check out this link.

App Permissions

When installing an app you are presented with the types of permissions that the app requires. These permissions can include things such as gathering your location information, modifying your contacts, permission to send SMS messages on your behalf, etc. Make sure that the required permissions make sense. Research apps that exhibit suspicious permissions to verify legitimacy.

Unsecured Wifi

Unprotected Wifi networks, particularly in public places, can be dangerous. This is because you are connecting without knowing who else could be on the network. A malicious person can capture information, including credentials as they travel across the network.

These sorts of attacks are mostly useful for harvesting unencrypted credentials, but just because a connection is encrypted does not necessarily mean it is safe.

There are dedicated devices that attackers can use to setup rogue access points for the sole purpose of harvesting information: https://www.wifipineapple.com/.
